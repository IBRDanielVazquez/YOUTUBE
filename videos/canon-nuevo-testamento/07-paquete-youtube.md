### Descripción 1 (larga, SEO)

For over 1,600 years, people have repeated the same claim: "a church council decided which books belong in the Bible." It's one of the most common myths about the New Testament — and it isn't true.

This documentary follows the actual evidence trail behind the formation of the New Testament canon: from Paul's letters circulating separately in the 1st century, through Marcion's reduced canon in Rome (c. 144 AD), Irenaeus of Lyon's argument for exactly four Gospels (c. 180 AD), the disputed dating of the Muratorian Fragment, Origen and Eusebius's classification of recognized, disputed, and spurious writings, all the way to Athanasius of Alexandria's Festal Letter 39 (367 AD) — the first surviving document to list the exact 27 books of today's New Testament — and its regional ratification at the Councils of Hippo (393) and Carthage (397/419).

Along the way, we address directly the popular myth that the Council of Nicaea (325 AD) decided the biblical canon — it didn't, and we show you exactly why, using the surviving records. We also cover the Nag Hammadi discovery (1945) and why texts like the Gospel of Thomas never became part of the New Testament.

Every claim in this documentary is labeled by evidentiary status — Fact, Hypothesis, Interpretation, Tradition, or Academic Consensus — including real, ongoing academic debates (such as the disputed dating of the Muratorian Fragment between the 2nd and 4th centuries). This is not a devotional or apologetic video, and it does not take a denominational side — it is a historical account built on primary sources and peer-reviewed scholarship (Bruce Metzger, Lee Martin McDonald, Michael J. Kruger, Bart D. Ehrman, David Trobisch, among others).
